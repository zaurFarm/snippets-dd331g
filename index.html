The Perfect Two-Watch Collection: A Classic Submariner and a New Jubilee

The concept of a two-watch collection assumes you want maximum coverage with minimum redundancy. One watch for active, outdoor, and casual settings. One watch for everything else. If both happen to be Rolex, the pairing that covers the widest range is a Submariner and a Datejust on Jubilee bracelet.

The Submariner half

The Submariner is a 300-meter dive watch that has been worn everywhere from actual ocean floors to boardrooms. The 2026 reference maintains the 41mm Oystersteel case with Cerachrom bezel and Oyster bracelet. It is rated for professional diving but sized and finished well enough for a suit. The black dial and bezel combination is the most versatile — it matches everything and attracts attention from no one who is not already a watch person.

The Jubilee Datejust half

The Datejust on a Jubilee bracelet is the dressier counterpart. The five-link bracelet catches light in a way the Submariner's Oyster bracelet does not, giving it presence at dinner, at events, and in any context where you want the watch to be noticed. The 2026 colorful dial options — particularly the blue sunburst or champagne — add personality without compromising versatility.

Why this pairing works

  • Complete situational coverage — dive watch for casual, Jubilee for formal
  • Different bracelets ensure each watch feels distinct on the wrist
  • Both hold value exceptionally well on the secondary market
  • The Submariner's tool-watch DNA contrasts with the Datejust's dressy elegance
  • Combined retail sits below most single high-complication watches

The upgrade path

Starting with these two leaves room to grow. A GMT-Master II adds a travel complication. A Day-Date adds a precious metal dress watch. A Daytona adds a chronograph. But many collectors who start with the Submariner-plus-Datejust pairing find they never feel underserved — the two watches simply cover too much ground to justify adding more unless the desire is purely emotional. And emotional is a perfectly valid reason to buy another watch.
